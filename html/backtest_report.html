Pure CANSLIM Backtest Results

Results of testing one implementation of CANSLIM versus two benchmark strategies:

- Market Only Strategy: Fully invests in SPY without any adjustments.

- Risk-Managed Market Strategy: Dynamically allocates between SPY and BIL based on the M in CANSLIM below.

Oâ€™Neil is careful not to explicitly define CANSLIM. Our version works by insisting each of these be true (allowing at most six stocks per quarter)

Letter Name Description / Implementation
C Current Quarterly Earnings EPS growth > 20% YOY
A Annual Earnings Growth 5% annual EPS growth threshold
N New High 52-week high breakout
S Supply & Demand Volume factor > average daily volume
L Leader / Laggard Relative strength > market
I Institutional Sponsorship A/D ratio > 1.0 (50-day window)
M Market Direction 50-day MA > 200-day MA

The backtest started with an initial investment of $100,000, covering just under 16 years (2008-12-26 to 2024-12-06) rebalanced quarterly. We also tested weekly rebalancing, but it always performed worse. Things go similarly for O'Neil's FFTY ETF, a weekly, rules-based, computer-generated stock index compiled and published by Investor's Business Daily.
